Foundations

Standing on the Shoulders of Failures

Sentinel Prime did not appear from nowhere. It is the synthesis of a decade of open privacy work — projects that proved what was possible, exposed what was missing, and left clear lessons for anyone willing to build the whole stack.

Read the bricks Explore the ecosystem

Mission

Sentinel Prime exists to prove that privacy-first technology can be powerful, beautiful, and complete — without surveillance economics, without opaque cloud dependency, and without asking users to assemble a dozen half-solutions themselves.

We build on-device AI, a hardened Android operating system, privacy hardware, and developer tools that respect the same principles: local execution, explicit consent, auditable architecture.

AI built for people, not corporations. That is not a tagline. It is a constraint on every product decision we make.

“I combined everyone else's failures.”
— Paul Green, Founder, Sentinel Prime Inc.

That line is not a dismissal of the projects that came before. Each “failure” here means an incomplete outcome — not a lack of integrity or effort. GrapheneOS hardened Android but did not ship a consumer phone. Murena de-Googled mobile but could not unify AI and OS development. Purism pursued hardware sovereignty at a scale most people could not reach. CalyxOS made privacy accessible but stopped short of a full product company. Pine64 opened hardware but left the software stack fragmented.

Sentinel's job is synthesis: learn honestly, credit openly, and build the tower those bricks were always pointing toward.

SEC

Brick I

GrapheneOS

Lesson: security architecture first

GrapheneOS set the technical bar for what a hardened Android baseline can be — sandboxing discipline, exploit mitigation, and a security culture that treats the OS as infrastructure, not marketing.

SentinelOS is built on that foundation. We do not reinvent security theater. We inherit serious engineering and extend it toward a complete consumer and developer experience.

UX

Brick II

Murena / eOS

Lesson: privacy must feel normal

Murena showed that de-Googled mobile does not have to feel like a compromise. BlissLauncher and the eOS ecosystem proved users want clean interfaces, not ideological suffering.

SentinelOS integrates BlissLauncher3 for that same clarity — modern, calm, and usable on day one. Privacy should feel like upgrade, not exile.

HW

Brick III

Purism

Lesson: hardware is part of the promise

Purism pushed the industry to ask who controls the device — firmware, radios, and supply chain included. That question belongs at the center of any serious privacy company.

Sentinel X is our answer at practical scale: a phone you can actually buy, pre-loaded with SentinelOS, without carrier surveillance defaults or Big Tech lock-in.

ACC

Brick IV

CalyxOS

Lesson: accessibility scales trust

CalyxOS made privacy Android approachable — realistic device support, sensible defaults, and a focus on people who need protection without becoming systems administrators.

Sentinel products follow that same instinct: powerful underneath, simple on the surface. Guardian for security professionals. Personal AI for everyone else. Same ethics, different depth.

OSS

Brick V

Pine64

Lesson: open hardware invites builders

Pine64 democratized experimentation — affordable boards, community momentum, and proof that open hardware culture could survive outside corporate R&D labs.

Forge and Forge Lite extend that builder ethic: local AI that compiles real Android artifacts, flashes real devices, and keeps the toolchain on your machine — not ours.

Closing vision

The future of computing does not have to be a trade between capability and dignity. It should feel impossible in the best way — dimensional, precise, human — and still be understandable when you look closely.

Sentinel Prime is building that future one layer at a time: honest about where we came from, direct about what we ship, and committed to technology you can inspect, own, and trust.
